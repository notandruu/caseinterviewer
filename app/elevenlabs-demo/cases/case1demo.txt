Case Name: Summit Snacks Profit Decline Case

Background  
Summit Snacks is a mid sized American snack manufacturer headquartered in Denver, Colorado. They sell three main product lines: protein bars, granola bites, and trail mix. Their biggest customers are national grocery chains and convenience stores. Summit has been profitable for the past 7 years and grew revenue by 12 percent annually from 2020 to 2023.

Current Situation  
In Q3 2025, Summit Snacks reported a 9 percent drop in overall profit even though revenue stayed flat. The CEO wants to understand what is causing the decline and whether it is operational, pricing, or market driven.  
The company recently opened a new distribution hub in Kansas City to shorten delivery routes for the Midwest and Southeast. They also renegotiated contracts with two logistics partners.

Key Facts  
- Revenue in Q3 2025: 38 million USD (flat vs Q3 2024).  
- Overall profit margin dropped from 14 percent to 9 percent.  
- Protein bars grew 6 percent year over year.  
- Granola bites declined 4 percent year over year.  
- Trail mix stayed flat but now has higher return rates due to packaging issues.  
- The new Kansas City distribution hub required 3.1 million USD in upfront setup.  
- Transportation costs increased by 18 percent after switching logistics partners.  
- Customer complaints about late deliveries rose by 22 percent.  
- No major competitors changed pricing or launched significant products during this period.

Client Objective  
Identify the root cause of the profit margin decline and recommend actions to restore margins to at least 14 percent within two quarters.
